About Me

Background

Hello! I'm Federico, a dedicated computer science student at Virginia Tech with an anticipated graduation date of December 2024. Ever since I've been introduced to programming, I have been completely captivated by its power to solve problems and create innovative solutions. During my time at Virginia Tech, I have never stopped exploring new concepts and emerging technologies to expand my knowledge and skills. From algorithms and data structures to artificial intelligence and systems programming, I have always welcomed every challenge this major has brought upon me. My ultimate goal is to leverage my education and experiences to create a mutually beneficial relationship between myself, a company, and consumers. I'm excited to embark on this journey of growth and discovery!

My experiences

My software engineering journey began at W.T. Woodson High School in Fairfax, VA. Here, I was introduced to computer science through courses I took during my Junior and Senior year; once I got accepted into Virginia Tech, I had no doubt that I would choose to major in Computer Science. Througout my first three years at Virginia Tech, I completed my required courses: Data Structures and Algorithms, Computer Organization, various math and science courses, Computer Systems, and some interesting CS related electives; I credit these classes for giving me the fundemental knowledge that is necessary to succeed in this field. After my Junior year, I was lucky enough to have gotten an internship offer at Amazon. This internship was my most formative experience to date. I was able to work alongside some of the best engineers I've ever met and I learned how to use many technologies that I had no previous exposure to. However, the biggest lesson I learned was: there is a difference between being a good programmer and a good engineer, a good engineer practices daily habits that enable large teams to work together and sees his or her problem scope further out than just the code they are typing. As I am finishing up my college education, I have picked up a job as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for Introduction to Artificial Intelligence; this is a great way for me to exercise my ability to explain concepts from different angles. Moving forward, I am in the search for an internship for the summer of 2024.
